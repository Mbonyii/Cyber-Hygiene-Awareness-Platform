Password Safety

Use long, unique passphrases. A password manager helps you avoid reuse and keeps credentials encrypted. Turn on multi-factor authentication for accounts that matter most.

Do
  • Use 3–4 random words or a password manager
  • Enable MFA with an authenticator app
  • Rotate passwords if you suspect a breach
Avoid
  • Reusing passwords across sites
  • Saving passwords in plain text or notes
  • Sharing credentials over email or chat
Quick Wins
  • Turn on MFA for email and banking first
  • Update old passwords flagged in data leaks
  • Use device biometrics for convenience

Phishing Examples Learn to spot the tells

These samples are safe. Hover or tap to reveal why they are risky.

Email Subject: "Urgent: Account Verification"
Phishing

"Dear user, your account will be suspended in 2 hours. Click here to keep it active."

Red flags: urgency, generic greeting, and a link that hides its destination.
SMS: "Delivery failed—update address"
Phishing

"We tried to deliver your package. Confirm your address and payment here."

Red flags: unexpected payment request, shortened link, and a sense of urgency.

Online Safety Tips

Stay Updated

Install updates for your OS, browser, and apps to patch vulnerabilities.

Verify Before You Trust

Check sender addresses, URLs, and spelling before entering credentials.

Limit Sharing

Avoid posting personal details publicly; attackers can reuse them in scams.
